PROCESS

Building Structure

The original website featured a three-column structure that lacked clear organization, making it challenging to envision how it could be made responsive. As a result, responsiveness was not fully addressed.

The styling could benefit from a refresh to create more consistency. For instance, the homepage used a variety of fonts, button styles, and colors, which made it difficult to maintain a cohesive look. Additionally, the images could be improved for better quality and lighting.

ü™ü Reorganizing the Grid

I simplified the layout from 3‚Üí2 column to improve readability and better highlight the images. The redesign follows a more structured grid system, enhancing the visual hierarchy for clearer navigation.

üñáÔ∏è Grouping Bakery Items

I also reorganized the content to better highlight the bakery‚Äôs signature cakes, grouping them into dedicated sections with clear titles. This focused approach ensures that each product is presented in an organized and easily-scannable way.

üìñ Storytelling

Additionally, I prioritized telling a cohesive story at the beginning of the site, providing a logical flow that guides the user rather than scattering disjointed information throughout the image gallery. This helped create a more engaging and visually coherent experience for visitors.

The website is on its way to becoming fully responsive!

Restructuring the Navigation

In addition to the visual design issues, the website‚Äôs navigation was very cluttered with 10 items crammed into the menu, reflecting a lack of hierarchy. Many of these felt secondary against the main services the bakery is offering.

Prioritizing Links

I ensured that only the most prominent items were displayed upfront, while secondary items were moved to the hamburger menu for a cleaner, more streamlined experience.

Other Changes from Lo-fi to Hi-fi

I emphasized the bakery‚Äôs branding language, such as ‚ÄúMade with Butter and Love,‚Äù in the hero section so that it‚Äôs visible as soon as the website loads, which wasn‚Äôt addressed in the lo-fi. This creates a friendlier, more inviting tone right from the start.

In the lo-fi design, crucial information, such as the bakery hours, was placed at the very bottom of the page. However, in the high-fi, I moved this information right after the hero section for better accessibility and visibility.

Striking the Right Balance

While working the website, a friend pointed out that my design felt more like a modern bakery rather than the family-owned Edelweiss Bakery.

This raised a crucial question:

How can I maintain the essence of the original website?

After discussing this with another designer, I realized that completely preserving the old website's essence was challenging due to the numerous issues my redesign aimed to resolve. However, my redesign was already on the right track, as it retained the same copy, along with warm images that evoked similar feelings as the original website.

To balance both perspectives on the visual front, I made sure to incorporate some existing elements from the old site, such as a script font and select colors, to better reflect the bakery's original character.

Refreshing Edelweiss's Brand Identity

The style guide preserves elements of the old website‚Äôs warm color palette and typography. The new design uses just two fonts, compared to the original seven, with Kaushan Script applied sparingly for the logo and hero text to reinforce Edelweiss's brand identity. Additionally, I simplified the UI by decreasing the number of buttons from five to two to create consistency.
