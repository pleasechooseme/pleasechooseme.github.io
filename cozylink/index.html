CONTEXT

What is CozyLink?

CozyLink is a student startup aimed to tackle the fragmented, inefficient, and often unreliable rental process that many college students face with an all-in-one housing app.

As the founding designer, I designed the entire mobile app from 0-1 including creating the logo, design system, and end-to-end flows over the course of two semesters. I collaborated with our founders and engineers on a weekly basis to present my work and align our vision for the app.

SOLUTION

Core Experiences

CozyLink is an all-in-one housing app that simplifies the rental process by providing students with full transparency on rental costs and availability. We connect students with other verified users, including previous tenants, interested tenants, and property listers, and offer roommate matching features to create a more trustworthy and streamlined housing experience.

01. Start browsing in four taps

Browse properties as a guest and start viewing immediately, but to access roommate profiles, you'll need to create an account and set up a profile for verification purposes.

02. Or take your time to complete your profile

Use our branding to personalize your profile, either by uploading your photo or selecting a custom avatar.

03. Know exactly what to expect upfront

Get full transparency on rental expenses, including utility and Wi-Fi fees, laundry options, furniture details, and room availability. Inquire about open rooms, explore the interactive map, and clearly see what’s included or excluded in each property.

04. Map key locations and get automatic distance calculations

Use the interactive map to calculate distances between the property and key locations based on your mode of transit, all without leaving the app.

05. Connect directly with potential roommates and others

Easily form a housing group by reaching out to current and previous tenants, as well as the landlord, to ask any questions about the property.

06. Access comprehensive property insights

View overall ratings, read comments, and ask anonymous questions about the property—answered exclusively by verified tenants and landlords. Explore the landlord’s profile for additional reviews and discover their other properties.

07. Find your ideal roommate with our matching feature

Create a roommate profile to start your search. Share some personality on your profile card, then go in-depth into your lifestyle and preferences in the next and final step.

08. Star properties and organize them

Save properties into custom folders based on your preferences, and share them with others in your housing group.

RESEARCH

Research Methods

To kick off the project, I collaborated with the founders and our design strategist to understand students’ experiences with the housing rental process, validating the need for our app.

📝 65 Survey Responses

Gain insights into student preferences between dorms and off-campus housing, current methods they use, and frustrations with the process.

🤝 10 Individual Interviews

Delve deeper into students' personal needs to identify the in-app features they desire most.

💻 Secondary Research

Explore similar apps in the market to understand user experiences, including feedback from listers, for valuable guidance.

We discovered that:

Students are excited about living off-campus, but feel frustrated and uncertain due to the lack of formal modes of searching for roommates and rentals and transparency on landlords and costs.

Key Findings

College students are enthusiastic to live off-campus as a way to gain greater independence. However, they are currently frustrated by the disjointed methods of searching for rentals and communicating with all parties involved.

77.8% of students discover housing options primarily through informal ways like friends and social media, but they wish for roommate matching apps.

Lessors struggle to find suitable tenants while renters struggle to find detailed information about landlords and rentals, often resulting in not being able to sublet or compromising prices to find people.

Competitive Analysis

To supplement my primary research, I analyzed 6 mobile apps and 1 website before starting initial concepts. While my intention was to focus on these platforms’ onboarding flows, I extracted far more useful information to guide my designs and position our app more favorably to our users.

I ended up using Airbnb’s mobile app as my main reference since they encompassed the most features that aligned with CozyLink’s goals.

PROCESS

Mapping out Desired Features

Gathering insights from our interviewees, I organized the features users mentioned wanting in our app and grouped them under key concepts. The dots below the gray sticky notes represent the frequency these features were mentioned.

Overall, users want more transparency on rentals, such as additional fees outside of the total rent and the ability to verify the landlord or lister of each property. Users are also looking for easier ways to ensure compatibility and connect with prospective roommates. Finally, they want to maximize in-app communication to avoid using multiple apps.

Iterations

Lo-fi

From our discussions, we initially tailored the app for students looking for housing and planning to sublease. As a result, I combined both search and listing features into one comprehensive homepage, including rental search, roommates, subleasing, and subletting.

After team review, I realized I had misunderstood the roommate board as being for users with secured rentals, rather than those forming housing groups. Navigation was unclear, and there was no easy way to access the roommate questionnaire or rental posting form. I also noticed overlap between the roommates and landing pages, and confusion around the distinction between subleasing and subletting.

Mid-fi

I refined the design by adding more context to the title sections and creating a toggle for "Rental" and "Lister Details" on the rental details page. I also introduced features like an interactive map, an amenities list, and a Q&A board.

To address the need for transparency, I moved roommate profiles to the lister details section and added landlord and tenant information. Finally, I updated the fixed footer to include quick actions for requesting a roommate or scheduling a tour.

Although each iteration brought improvements, I still had doubts about the app's structure.

Was dedicating an entire navigation item for creating roommate profiles or rental posts necessary, given that students typically use these features only once? Additionally, the footer on the rental details page, with options like "Request Roommate" and "Arrange Tour," created confusion by merging student-specific and lister-specific features.

⚠️ Overcoming a Roadblock: Redesigning for Diverse User Needs

Our team's goal was to combine listing and renting functionalities into a single account, and I designed with this approach in mind. However, this solution didn’t effectively address the diverse needs of our users. While my iterations met the needs of renters, they struggled to serve all user types—renter, sublessor, and landlord—due to the challenges of consolidating multiple functionalities into one account.

After revisiting my competitor analysis, I realized a complete reorganization of the app was necessary.

All seven platforms I analyzed had separate renter/guest and lister/host accounts, which highlighted the need for this distinction. Although I didn't work on the lister account, I focused on tailoring the renter account to better meet their specific needs.

Reimagining the Core Experience

After multiple discussions with the team about the restructure, we created a dedicated renter navigation. I replaced the "Create" navigation item with a "Community" item to offer additional support to young renters, boosting their confidence in the rental process and helping them transition to full independence.

With a clearer direction in place, I moved forward with the final high-fidelity designs, which became the final stage of my work on the project.

01. Build Confidence in the Rental Process

Clear Property Information at a Glance

Users can quickly access key details, such as room availability and what's included in the rental, directly from the homepage. By tapping into the property card, they can view more in-depth information about the listing, including details on tenants, landlords, and comprehensive costs (e.g., utilities and Wi-Fi), ensuring transparency without hidden fees.

Access Reviews and Ask Questions

Users can read reviews from verified tenants—those who have actually lived in the property—to gain valuable insights into their experiences. Additionally, they can post questions in the Q&A section to gather more information and explore other properties that may better meet their needs.

02. Streamline and Centralize the Search

In-App Messaging with the Right People

Users can easily contact interested tenants, previous tenants, and landlords directly within the app, streamlining communication and ensuring they connect with the right people at the right time.

My Places

I designed an interactive map that allows users to add their locations and automatically calculates distances and commute times. This feature eliminates the need to leave the app and use an external map, simplifying the process and making it more convenient. The feature was received very positively by the team!

03. Make the Search More Enjoyable

Social Media-Like Interactions

Inspired by social media, users can send friend-like requests to initiate connections, making the process more engaging and personable.

Sending a Message Request

This feature allows users to express interest in interacting with someone, fostering community engagement while offering a controlled way to initiate communication.

Responding to a Message Request

Users can manage their interactions by only responding to requests from people they are interested in or feel comfortable with, ensuring greater privacy and safety by filtering communication.

Additional Roommate Card Builder Step

The roommate profile card showcases personality, allowing users to highlight key aspects of who they are and what they’re looking for in a roommate, making the search process more enjoyable and relatable.

LEARNINGS

Takeaways

  1. Don't strive for perfection: My younger self would have never thought I'd say this. However, our strive for perfection severely hindered our progress. We aimed to create a flawless product rather than focusing on a MVP and allowing user testing to guide the rest of the product. We should have prioritized key features and planned releases more effectively instead of trying to launch everything at once.
  2. Focus on the overall experience: I spent an excessive amount of time on minor design aesthetics rather than on the overall user experience. This led to missing important edge cases and interaction details during prototyping. It’s crucial to balance visual design with usability to ensure a comprehensive and functional user experience.
  3. Align on goals early: We often found ourselves misaligned on goals and lacked a comprehensive brainstorming session at the beginning of the project. Holding such a session would have helped us clarify objectives and set a unified direction. I would advocate for early goal alignment and collaborative planning in future projects.
  4. Invest in discovery and definition: I would allocate more time to the discovery and definition phases of a project. This includes interviewing both renters and landlords, developing detailed user flows, and creating wireframes before moving on to mid to high-fidelity designs. Thorough exploration and definition of user needs and workflows are essential for creating a well-rounded and effective design.
